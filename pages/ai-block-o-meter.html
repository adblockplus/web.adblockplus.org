How we count Methodology, refresh cadence & honest disclosure

1 What counts as an AI-platform block

An ad is counted when an Adblock Plus or AdBlock filter rule fires on a request originating from a known AI platform domain (e.g. chatgpt.com, openai.com) and matches a creative slot, sponsored card, or third-party ad-tech endpoint.

2 How we isolate ChatGPT traffic

We attribute requests by the page-context origin reported by the browser's filtering API. Requests fired from a ChatGPT tab are tagged separately from general web browsing — the tag never includes URL paths, prompts, or any user content.

3 Refresh cadence

Counters above update with anonymised aggregate telemetry on a rolling weekly window. The "this week" tile resets every Monday 00:00 UTC. Backfills for a previous week are sometimes published the following Friday.

4 What we deliberately don't collect

We never log URLs, page contents, prompts, AI responses, or anything that could identify you. The counter is a sum of events, not a record of sessions. See our privacy policy.

⚠

Honest disclosure. Ads embedded inside AI-generated responses — sponsored phrasing or product placements served as part of the model output rather than a separate ad slot — are not yet measurable by filter-level telemetry. The counters above reflect ad requests blocked, not in-response promotions. We're researching ways to detect the latter; until then, treat this as a floor, not a ceiling.
